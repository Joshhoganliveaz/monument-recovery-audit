Section 03

Critical Issues

These issues are actively limiting search visibility and should be addressed first.

1. No Schema Markup (Structured Data) High Impact

What we found: The site has an empty <script type="application/ld+json"> tag from the GatherUp review widget, but no actual structured data is being output. There is no MedicalOrganization, LocalBusiness, FAQPage, or Article schema on any page.

Why it matters: Schema markup is how you explicitly tell Google and AI systems "we are a licensed medical facility at this address with these services." Without it, search engines have to guess. For healthcare sites, this directly impacts:

  • Eligibility for rich results (enhanced search listings with ratings, address, phone)
  • Google Business Profile integration with the website
  • AI systems' ability to extract and cite facility details accurately
  • Knowledge Panel population and accuracy

Content with proper schema shows 30-40% higher AI visibility according to current research.

Recommended Fix

Add the following schema types across the site:

  • MedicalOrganization + LocalBusiness -- sitewide in the global header
  • FAQPage -- on treatment pages and blog posts that contain Q&A content
  • Article / BlogPosting -- on all blog posts (with author, date, publisher)
  • MedicalWebPage -- on treatment service pages

In HubSpot, this can be added via the theme's global header or through a custom coded module. A copy-paste-ready JSON-LD block is included in the Appendix.

2. No Author Attribution on Blog Content High Impact

What we found: All 40+ blog posts are attributed to "Monument Recovery" as an organization. No individual author names, credentials, or bios appear on any post.

Why it matters: For YMYL (healthcare) content, Google's Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines explicitly look for content "created by individuals with appropriate expertise or qualifications." Addiction treatment is one of the most scrutinized categories. Google wants to see:

  • Who wrote this content? (Named author with visible credentials)
  • Are they qualified? (Licensed therapist, LCSW, MD, etc.)
  • Was clinical content medically reviewed? (A "Reviewed by" badge)

Without this, Google treats blog content as lower-authority, and AI systems are less likely to cite it as a source because they can't verify expertise.

Recommended Fix
  • Assign named authors from the clinical team (staff listed on /addiction-recovery-team)
  • Add author bio boxes to blog posts with name, title, credentials, and photo
  • Add "Medically Reviewed by [Name], [Credential]" badges to clinical content
  • Create individual author archive pages so Google can connect expertise to content
3. Missing Meta Descriptions on Inner Pages High Impact

What we found: The homepage has a meta description, but key inner pages are missing them, including Treatment Services, About, and the Blog listing page.

Why it matters: The meta description is what appears as the snippet text beneath your page title in Google search results. When it's missing, Google auto-generates one by pulling random text from the page -- often resulting in an awkward, incomplete snippet that doesn't compel clicks. Low click-through rates signal to Google that the result isn't relevant, which can gradually lower rankings.

Recommended Fix

Write unique meta descriptions (150-160 characters) for every page. Each should include the primary keyword, a clear value statement, and a reason to click. Examples:

  • Treatment Services: "Monument Recovery offers residential, PHP, IOP, and virtual outpatient addiction treatment in Mesa, AZ. Individualized plans for substance use and mental health."
  • About: "Learn about Monument Recovery's evidence-based approach to addiction treatment in Mesa, Arizona. Accredited facility with licensed therapists and holistic care."
  • Blog: "Recovery resources, treatment guides, and expert insights from Monument Recovery's clinical team in Mesa, AZ."
Section 04

Moderate Issues

These issues reduce performance but aren't as urgent as the critical items above.

4. Multiple H1 Tags on Homepage Medium Impact

What we found: The homepage has two H1 tags: "Addiction Treatment & Rehab in Arizona" and "Build Your Best Life." Each page should have exactly one H1 that clearly signals the page's primary topic to search engines.

Recommended Fix

Keep "Addiction Treatment & Rehab in Arizona" as the H1. Change "Build Your Best Life" to an H2.

5. Image Alt Text Missing or Generic Medium Impact

What we found: Many images use generic filenames as alt text (e.g., "DSC02519-1.jpg", "24-MMT-0535-Website2-1.png") or appear to have no alt text at all.

Why it matters: Alt text helps Google understand image content for image search rankings and accessibility compliance. Descriptive alt text like "Monument Recovery residential treatment common area in Mesa Arizona" reinforces location relevance and helps rank in image search.

Recommended Fix

Audit all images sitewide. Replace generic filenames with descriptive alt text that includes relevant keywords naturally. Focus on facility photos, team photos, and treatment program images first.

6. Homepage Title Could Be Stronger Medium Impact

Current: "Monument Recovery | Rehab in Arizona"

Issue: "Arizona" is broad. Most people searching for treatment search by city. The title misses the "Mesa" keyword entirely.

Recommended Fix

Suggested: "Addiction Treatment & Rehab in Mesa, AZ | Monument Recovery" -- puts the primary keyword first, includes the city name, keeps the brand at the end.

7. Treatment Service Pages Are Thin Medium Impact

What we found: The Treatment Services page has approximately 800-900 words. Individual treatment pages appear similarly thin. Competitive rehab pages in the Mesa/Phoenix market typically run 1,500-2,500+ words.

Why it matters: Google evaluates whether content provides sufficient depth to answer the searcher's question. For queries like "PHP addiction treatment Arizona," ranking pages comprehensively cover what PHP involves, who it's for, daily schedule, duration, insurance, and outcomes.

Recommended Fix

Expand each treatment page to 1,500+ words covering: program structure, daily schedule, therapeutic modalities, duration, who it's best for, insurance/cost info, and frequently asked questions.

Key AI-SEO Recommendations

A. Add FAQ Sections to Treatment Pages

Each treatment page should have a 5-8 question FAQ section at the bottom, answering the exact questions families ask. Mark these up with FAQPage schema. Example questions for the PHP page:

  • "How many hours per day is PHP treatment?"
  • "What's the difference between PHP and IOP?"
  • "Does insurance cover PHP at Monument Recovery?"
  • "How long does a PHP program typically last?"
  • "Can I live at home during PHP?"

B. Add Definition Blocks to Service Page Openings

The first paragraph of every treatment page should be a clear, self-contained definition (40-60 words) that directly answers "What is [this treatment]?" This is the passage AI systems extract and cite. Currently, service pages lead with marketing language rather than direct answers.

C. Incorporate Statistics with Cited Sources

Add data points from authoritative sources (SAMHSA, NIDA, peer-reviewed studies) throughout treatment pages. Research shows including cited statistics increases AI citation rates by approximately 37-40%.

D. Build a Comparison Table: Levels of Care

A clear side-by-side table (Residential vs PHP vs IOP vs Virtual IOP) covering hours/week, typical duration, structure level, who it's best for, and cost range. This single table could capture "[X] vs [Y]" treatment queries.

E. Verify Third-Party Directory Presence

AI systems frequently cite third-party sources rather than the facility's own website. Ensure Monument Recovery is listed and current on:

  • SAMHSA Treatment Locator (findtreatment.gov)
  • Google Business Profile (optimized with photos, Q&A, regular posts)
  • Psychology Today directory
  • Rehabs.com and similar treatment directories
